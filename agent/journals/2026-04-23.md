## 14:XX
Eric added notes from the Thu Apr 23 1pm Options Pricing Methodology meeting.

Key outcomes:

**Deribit inverse pricing** — confirmed NOT appropriate for their linear options pricing. Suley's concerns validated: pin risk at expiry, price divergence around expiry, and interest rate differential. Deribit uses the futures price with same expiry as the forward (not spot + interest rate), which creates a model gap when translating to their linear (USDC-settled) pricing.

**M2M pricing source** — Haruko confirmed as the source. Mechanism: book a synthetic trade into a test account for a bilateral option (linear), Haruko generates a price from its vol surface. Chris Davidson has a test account to work with. Vol surface model is standard (Black-76 / "Black 88"). Bob and Suley both want a 3rd-party validated source, not internal models. Risk team responsible for model validation.

**Expiry settlement price** — resolved: Deribit index price − strike, using a 30-minute TWAP (1-minute slices) to prevent manipulation. This is the same mechanism Deribit itself uses for expiry settlement. This was the open item blocking Eric's eng estimate for expiry work.

**Hedge vs price relationship** — no direct constrained relationship between the hedge price and the price shown to the client. May be tunable over time but no guarantees. This is expected behavior for an OTC dealer.

**Long-term vision** — unified model between pricing (M2M) and quoting (desk), with a validated base model and front-office discretion for markups. Same model validated by risk, front-office tweaks it for client quoting. Easier from audit/regulatory perspective.
