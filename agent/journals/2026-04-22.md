### Stablecoin Cross-Entity — Three-trade model confirmed

**Flow (counterparty sells USDC → gets USD in Prime):**
1. Counterparty sells USDC to Digital
2. Digital sells USDC to LLC (USDC sent to LLC's BitGo wallet)
3. LLC settles USD into Digital's prime account; Digital credits counterparty's account
Result: USDC lands as USD in counterparty's LLC account

**LLC holds USDC inventory with 2% capital haircut.** When LLC wants to exit, sells back to Digital who routes through market makers.

**Settlement sequencing (coin-first, prevents unfunded liability):**
1. Client USDC lands in Digital's designated BitGo deposit address
2. All bookings happen simultaneously: credit client, debit Digital, credit LLC with USDC, debit LLC with cash, credit Digital prime
3. Settlement cannot proceed until USDC confirmed received
- Digital→LLC: payment vs. payment at 4pm delivery
- Digital←LLC: delivery vs. payment (Digital delivers cash first)

**Wallet/SSI:**
- Each counterparty assigned specific USDC deposit address
- SSI table maps counterparty → send/receive addresses
- Matching engine expects specific qty from specific address on specific settlement date
- When qty doesn't match (e.g. 10.1 vs 10.0), trade won't match — manual resolution needed
- Institutional: multiple addresses per token for different purposes (funding prime vs. settling to own wallet)
- Active clients: one address per asset per network

**Open concern**: @dhammerich (Dalf) worried about synthetically crediting accounts before real cash. Brian Stern's sequencing (coin first, then credit, Digital as trusted counterparty to LLC) addresses this. All participants need to sign off on timing comfort.
